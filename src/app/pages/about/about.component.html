About

The German and Polish COVID-19 ForecastHub aims to bring together short-term forecasts of the further course of the pandemic in Germany and Poland. Our goal is to systematically compare different predictions, identify particularly reliable models and assess whether combinations of different forecasts can yield more reliable results.

The platform is run by members of the Chair of Statistics and Econometrics at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology and the Computational Statistics Group at Heidelberg Institute for Theoretical Studies, with support from the Signale Team at Robert Koch Institut. The project is developed in close exchange with the US COVID-19 ForecastHub run by the Reich Lab at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst.

This project is public and all forecasts can be downloaded from our github repository. More ample information on this project can be found in the associated Wiki.

The German and Polish COVID-19 Forecast Hub also forms the basis of a pre-registered forecast evaluation study on which we plan to report in 2021. The protocol is available here.

Forecast Targets

We currently focus on short-term forecasts of confirmed cases and deaths from COVID-19. Both targets are addressed both at an incident (i.e. weekly new cases and deaths) and a cumulative scale (total cases and deaths). New forecasts are issued every Monday/Tuesday and cover forecast horizons of one through four weeks into the future. A detailed description of forecast targets can be found here.

Forecast Evaluation

One goal of this platform is to compare the forecast performance of different models systematically. Results will be included on this website shortly. An outline of the methods used for evaluation can be found in this preprint.

Ensemble Building

Each week we combine the different forecasts submitted to the platform into so-called ensemble forecasts. Such combined forecasts are known to yield more reliable predictions in many domains and are becoming increasingly used in epidemiology. See here for a recent preprint on ensemble forecasts based on the US COVID-19 Forecast Hub. Technical details are provided in this wiki entry.
